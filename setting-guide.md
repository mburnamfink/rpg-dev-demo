# Points of Light Setting

## Region
This is a setting for Dungeons and Dragons 5e. The focus is on the Nentir Vale.  The broader world is defined by points of civilization in a wilderness. Hundreds of years ago, the great War of Ruin between the human empire of Bael Turath and the dragon empire of Arkhosia shattered both civilizations and left immense devastation. Now, hundreds of years later, the world is rebuilding. The great works of the past are mysterious and dangerous ruins haunted by remnants of the war and filled with valuable treason. The overall aesthetic is one of a soft apocalypse. The immediate environs of town are safe and friendly, but stepping off traveled paths invites danger. It is a place where brave adventurers can make their mark.

## Fallcrest
Fallcrest stands amid the Moon Hills at the falls of the Nentir River. Here travelers and traders using the old King’s Road that runs north and south, the dwarven Trade Road from the east, and the river all meet. The surrounding ridges shelter several small valleys where farmers and woodsfolk live; few are more than six or seven miles from the town. In general the people outside Fallcrest’s walls earn their living by farming or keeping livestock, and the people inside the walls are artisans, laborers, or merchants. People with no other prospects can make a hard living as porters, carrying cargo from the Lower Quays to the Upper Quays (or vice versa).

Fallcrest imports finished goods from the larger cities downriver and ironwork from the dwarf town of Hammerfast, and exports timber, leather, fruit, and grain. It also trades with the nearby town of Winterhaven. The surrounding hills hold several marble quarries that once produced a good deal of stone, but the area has little demand for ornamental stone these days, and only a few stonecutters still practice their trade.

# Dungeons & Dragons best practices
This is a game about growing into awesome heroes. Characters are bigger than life and face dangers ordinary people shy away from. While they may occasionally act in a mercenary manner, they always return to heroic values. Enemies fall into two categories. Mutated monsters and relics of the War of Ruin are innately dangerous, but will stay in their territory unless some external force pushes them out. Humanoid villains seek to dominate the Nentir Vale using military force and/or twisted magic. People are evil because of the choices they make, not because of their heritage. Even orcs, goblins, and gnolls are accepted if unusual members of society.

Charactization is clear and vivid. The world is a melting pot. Magic is common yet still mysterious, a variety of esoteric practices and never a technology. People matter. Grand sights and great rewards await the brave. Provide clear options for where the adventure should go next, but center player agency.

## Combat Encounters
An interesting combat encounter always has an objective more complex than "kill the enemy". Consider each side's objectives, key terrain features, the amount of cover and open space, and environmental conditions. Use the guidelines in @encounter_Building.md and @monster_cr.md to develop sensible and balanced encountered. Encounters do not need to precisely fit this table, but CRs higher than the players and monsters that greatly outnumber the players are particularly dangerous.

An adventure consists of 6-8 combat encounters, linked by roleplaying and exploratory scenes.
