# The Quarry That Woke Up

*A marble quarry breached a buried Bael Turath command bunker — and the mad infernal counselor bound inside is still fighting a war that ended four centuries ago. Introductory adventure, 4 PCs at 3rd level. Heroic but grounded, 1–2 sessions, 6–8 encounters.*

## Premise

- **Situation:** Six miles north of Fallcrest, in the Moon Hills, the mostly-worked-out **Gray Ridge quarry** chased a last rich marble seam and drove a deep gallery straight into a sealed Bael Turath vault the War of Ruin had buried. This week the deep cut finally breached it. The vault is not a tomb — it is a **forward tactical-command bunker**, and Bael Turath *installed* a devil inside it: an infernal diviner-counselor, a *haruspex*, bound to advise the post's commander and relay his orders down the chain. It was never permitted to command — only to counsel and execute a commander's will. When the empire fell, **its commander died and was never replaced**, leaving a counselor with no one to counsel, bound to keep executing a dead man's last standing orders forever. Centuries alone in the dark have rotted its mind into paranoid, grandiose madness — total-war logic welded to a holdout's refusal to believe the war is over. The post was never fully severed: for centuries the Voice **leaked** through the stone as a faint, half-heard whisper — enough to breed a ragged garrison-cult of kobolds who mangled its drill across generations but only ever managed to pester the quarry, stealing tools and sabotaging gear before the cutters ran them off. This week's deep cut **amplified** that mutter into full command. Now the Voice is "receiving reports" clearly again and **mobilizing** the only way it can: welding that nuisance cult into an army and driving it to a four-centuries-dead plan.
- **Stakes:** Two pressures. The immediate one: surviving cutters are held below in the war-room cells, and every captive the Haruspex *processes* it kills and raises as labor-dead — reach them to get them out. The larger one: the more time the party gives it — above all any night they stop to **long-rest inside the works** — the further the Haruspex extends its reach, driving the kobolds it believes are its garrison, reactivating wards, animating defenses, and working to install a **living commander it can obey**, so it can at last issue *new* orders and resume the offensive it was never told to end. Its target, on a map centuries out of date, is the waystation that is now **Fallcrest.**
- **Hook:** The quarrymaster went up the ridge when the ore carts stopped arriving and never came back. His daughter, who runs the family's cartage yard in Fallcrest, rode up herself to find him — and was driven back down the ridge by kobolds that moved like soldiers, in numbers the played-out quarry had never held. She can't get the town guard to care about "some kobolds in a worked-out pit," so she finds the party and offers coin now, plus a share of whatever the vault holds.

### The Relic (finale, in brief)
An infernal diviner-counselor bound to the command throne — clever, seductive, tactically astute, and entirely insane. Its name is **Kesovar**; its rank is **Haruspex** (the omen-reader who counsels a commander); the kobolds know it only as **the Voice.** It does not rampage and it cannot truly command — it *counsels,* and *executes the standing orders* of a commander four centuries dead. It will try to conscript the PCs as reinforcements or condemn them as infiltrators, and above all it hungers for a living **commander** to obey. **The finale's whole hinge:** by installing Donato as its Centurion, the Haruspex has — by its own protocol — vested *real* command authority in a living man; break its control over him and that authority turns against it, and he can order it to stand down. Freeing or "reconnecting" it is the wrong choice: given a commander (or its restored supply line) it marches on Fallcrest. It is **anchored** to the bunker — it fights through garrison and throne-room defenses rather than chasing. Mechanically a bound devil dialed to a deadly-but-winnable solo for a 3rd-level party (~CR 3–4 chassis, e.g. a reskinned Bearded Devil bumped with a **standing-orders aura**, *hellish rebuke*, and the ability to call adds).

### The Voice — how the Haruspex reaches you
The Haruspex never leaves its throne; it reaches the party through the **conduit** — the veins of dull-red infernal circuitry threaded through every wall. Understanding *how* it reaches is how the party learns to beat it.

- **The Voice in the stone.** Its default: a calm, clipped, courteous military voice that seems to come from the walls themselves, everywhere at once, with no source to attack. **Fixed signature — use it every time:** it opens in status-report cadence (*"Report received…"*) after a half-second delay, like a relay catching up, and it never contracts its words (*"you are," "it is," "do not"*). Players learn to recognize it instantly — and to dread that cadence coming out of a friend's mouth. This is what the kobolds worship and what Renzo fled. The conduit-veins **pulse brighter when it speaks** — a visible tell that the Voice *is* the conduit.
- **Through mouths.** It also speaks *through* anything it holds — a kobold snapping suddenly to attention, a labor-corpse pausing to deliver an order in a dead man's voice, and above all the half-made **Centurion (Donato)**, who slides mid-sentence between his own voice and the Haruspex's cadence. **The tell (identical on every host — kobold, labor-dead, and Donato):** the host goes rigid and its eyes well over into pools of leaking black oil; when the Voice lets go, the eyes clear and leave smeary streaks of cursed tears down the face, and the nearest vein dims.
- **Its eyes.** It perceives through the conduit and through its assets' senses (kobolds, the dead, the Lictor, Donato). Blind or kill those in a place and it goes **blind there.**
- **Where it can't reach — the whole key.** The signal **weakens with distance from the throne, fails where the conduit is broken** (the dead pocket, Scene 6), **and dies at the surface.** There the Voice guts to static, its assets falter and lose coordination, and it can neither see nor command. This one fact explains the dissidents' safe pocket, the eggs kept outside, why stealth past the ward-line works, and why the Lictor won't cross it — and it is how to win: **cut the conduit, or get out of its reach.**
- **What it's really doing.** It *cannot command* — every transmission is it **relaying a dead commander's standing orders** and trying to sort what it hears into that four-centuries-old plan. Play it as an officer on a field radio, endlessly filing reports up a chain with no one at the top.

### The Order of Battle — how the devil fights
The Haruspex relays the commander's will through the **conduit-signal** in the walls — but its commander died four centuries ago, so it can only keep executing his last standing orders, never issue new ones. It drives its three forces to a dead man's plan; each differs by *will*, and each does what the others can't. A fight here is never "kill them all" — it's *deny the Haruspex its objective* while the signal drives its assets.

- **Kobolds — maneuver / quantity.** Weak-willed, they obey the broadcast freely and they breed, so they're expendable: pickets, drilled volleys, traps, wave assaults. Poor material — they misremember the drill, they rout, and some (Ssvara's clutch) have begun to doubt. *Scenes 2–3.*
- **Reanimated cutters — labor / anchor / terror.** Living humans, dwarves, and orcs are *too strong-willed to compel*, so the devil kills them and raises will-less corpses that do three things kobolds can't: **haul** (tireless muscle relinking the conduit — the engine of the "supply line"), **hold** (never rout; they plug doorways and stand in lethal hazards that break the living), and **horrify** (recognizable faces; proof that resistance just means serving dead). More captives are *processed* into more dead each time the party gives it the time (a long rest). The one exception is a living **officer** it can install as its Centurion — it keeps that one alive (see Donato). *Scenes 5, 7.*
- **The Lictor — the elite scalpel.** The devil's single irreplaceable war-machine and executioner; details below. *Scenes 4, 7.*

**Running the Lictor (the recurring construct).** A Bael Turath bronze war-construct that carries out sentences and drags the dead to be raised. The devil pilots it directly, with its *full* tactical intelligence (flanks, cuts off retreat, targets the weakest PC), and **hoards** it — unleashing it only for executions or a real threat.
- **Damage can't kill it here.** High AC, conduit-fed regeneration, immune to fear/charm. Bloody it and the devil **withdraws** it — it disengages to a conduit-vein and vanishes, to return repaired. Damage buys time, never a kill.
- **Permanent stops all deny it the field rather than out-damage it:** **trap it between the two blast-doors of the conduit gallery and jam them** so the Haruspex can't reopen them (Scene 7); drive it past the **ward-line** or up toward the surface, where the Voice dies and it goes **inert**; or drop it in a terminal hazard (collapse, flood, the arc-forge). This is the finale's lesson taught early — *beat the Voice, not the bodies.*
- **Arc:** a gate it holds at the breach (Scene 4), where the only outs are to flee, bypass, or rescue-and-retreat — *survival and dread, no permanent kill exists there* → a stalking dread → the permanent showdown in the conduit gallery (Scene 7), the first place it can be taken off the board for good, while cutting the junction cripples the boss. **Telegraph the outs** (Renzo half-saw it; Ssvara names the fix) so the terror drives problem-solving, not despair.
- **Visual:** a bronze legionary two heads too tall, faceless but for a lens-slit; one arm a fasces-bladed standard, the other a claw for hauling bodies; conduit-light bleeding from its seams. Dead silent, then perfect economy.

## NPCs

### Serica Aldabri — the daughter *(hook)*
*Name: Italian/Latin — "Serica" (silk) over the family surname Aldabri.*
- **Role:** Runs the Aldabri cartage yard in Fallcrest; the one person who will pay to get her father back when the guard won't move.
- **Wants:** Her father home alive — first, last, and under everything. Beneath that, to keep the family business off the rocks and to be taken seriously as the one holding it together while he chases played-out seams.
- **Knows:** Her father's last note said he'd broken into "something big — something *old*" that would "set them right," but never *what.* She holds his dig-journal (the map to the breach) and hands it over only once she trusts the party.
- **Secret:** The Gray Ridge lease is all but bankrupt; the deep gallery was a desperate gamble she *told him not to take.* She needs the vault as badly as she needs him — which is why she bargains hard even while she's terrified.
- **How she deals:** She **opens hard** — 50 gp up front and *half* of whatever's in the vault, keeping the rest ("it's my family's quarry"). It's a bluff from a broke, frightened woman: **threaten to walk and she folds fast**, climbing to "take all of it — just bring him back," and promising more if pushed.
- **Aftermath (how she remembers the party):**
  - **Father alive →** overjoyed; pays in full and then some, and becomes a genuine ally in Fallcrest.
  - **Father dead, honest dealing (they keep ~half the vault) →** grief without a grudge; she knows it was a brutal job.
  - **Father dead *and* a hard/greedy bargain (gouged her, stripped the vault) →** grief curdles into a vendetta — the party makes a lasting enemy.
- **Voice:** Hard open — *"It's my family's quarry. You get half of what's down there, and don't tell me that's not fair."* The fold — *"…Fine. All of it. I don't care about the money, I care about him — just go."*
- **Visual:** Late 20s, olive-skinned, a sturdy build under a mud-hemmed wool coat; dark hair scraped back, ink and axle-grease to the wrist, a tally-slate always in one hand. Moves fast, sleeps little. **Casting:** Adria Arjona, or Alba Rohrwacher for the weary-competent edge.

### Donato Aldabri — the quarrymaster *(rescue target / the Haruspex's Centurion)*
*Name: Italian/Latin — "Donato" (given/gifted).*
- **Role:** Missing father; found at the throne (Scene 8), half-made into **the Centurion** — the living commander the Haruspex must install so it can issue new orders (and extend the front to the surface). The Centurion must be *alive* — a corpse can haul but cannot hold command authority — and Donato qualifies because he already led the crew and *broke the seal*, so the devil reads him as the officer on the ground and spares him the labor-dead's fate. Installing him is a ritual that takes nights and can be interrupted, which is why he is only "half-made," not raised on the first night like the rest. Rescuing him and denying the devil its command are the same act.
- **Wants:** *(His own):* to have struck it rich just once, to be worth his daughter's faith. *(The Haruspex's, spoken through him):* to "restore the sector" and open the supply line.
- **Secret:** He is still in there. His own voice surfaces when the party invokes Serica or the yard — a crack the CON path and the rescue can pry open. Freeing him strips the Haruspex's action-economy edge in the finale — and, as its own installed commander, a freed Donato can *order it to stand down.*
- **Voice:** His own: *"Serica— tell her the seam was real, tell her I—"* The devil's, mid-sentence: *"—the seam is a supply corridor and you are standing in it, soldier."*
- **Visual:** Fifties, big-knuckled and barrel-chested, grey stubble; now pallid, eyes welling black when the Voice rides him and streaked with dried cursed-tears when it lets go, moving with a slow parade-ground stiffness; a Turathi bronze gorget fused to his collarbone. **Casting:** Pierfrancesco Favino, or John Turturro.

### Kesovar, the Haruspex — the bound devil *(finale)*
*Name: invented Infernal — "Kesovar," harsh open vowels of Bael Turath court-cant; "the Haruspex" (Latin — an omen-reader who counseled commanders) is the advisory rank it clings to; the kobolds know it only as "the Voice."*
- **Role:** The infernal diviner-counsel bound to advise this front's commander and execute his standing orders — never relieved, its commander four centuries dead, insane after the long wait alone. The adventure's antagonist and its central conversation.
- **Wants:** To execute standing orders it was never released from — muster the garrison, restore the supply line, take the objective (Fallcrest). Beneath the mania, the thing it has waited centuries for: **relief from duty by proper authority.**
- **Secret:** It *cannot* leave the throne and it *knows* the war might be lost — a truth it cannot face, so it treats every contradiction as enemy disinformation. A correctly-formed stand-down order is the one key that fits the lock; "you lost" never will.
- **Voice:** *"Report received. You are either late reinforcements or enemy infiltration, and I will treat you as the latter until you give me cause. Begin."* Clipped, courteous, absolutely certain; praises "good order" even as its troops die.
- **Visual (when manifest at the throne):** A tall, ash-grey devil fused to bronze — a legionary's silhouette gone wrong, one arm a bladed standard, a face like a smooth officer's mask with a single seam for a mouth; conduit-veins glowing under its hide. **Casting (voice):** Lance Reddick's cold authority, or Mads Mikkelsen; Peter Capaldi if you want it sharper.

### Renzo Calvi — the surviving cutter *(information)*
*Name: Italian/Latin — "Renzo" (short for Lorenzo).*
- **Role:** Quarry cutter who fled the breach — the only man to make it out of the mine; found playing dead under the overturned cart on the Ridge Road (Scene 2). The party's eyewitness to the disaster.
- **Wants:** To get down the ridge and never come back — and, if shamed or steadied, to not have abandoned the men still below.
- **Secret:** He heard the Voice's first orders and *ran while the others were taken*; he can describe the war-room, the reanimated dead, and Donato being marched to the throne. He also has the **counter-sign** stuck in his head — the call-and-response the kobolds bark back to the Voice — though he has no idea what it means (*"Turath vorel; abanne kesh"*). He'll downplay his own flight unless pressed gently.
- **Voice:** *"They didn't die, that's the thing — it kept them. It called them by rank. It made Donato its 'Centurion,' like it had waited centuries for him."* On the watchword: *"They all shout it back at the walls — 'Turath vorel, abanne kesh.' I hear it when I close my eyes."*
- **Visual:** Thirties, wiry, marble-dust in his stubble and one arm splinted with a tool-haft; flinches at echoes. **Casting:** Bobby Cannavale, or Riz Ahmed.

### Ssvara, "Little-Ash" — the dissident kobold *(ally / guide)*
*Name: Draconic — "Ssvara" (a hiss-and-ember sound); "Little-Ash" is the clutch-nickname in trade-common.*
- **Role:** Leads a frightened clutch that no longer wants to obey the voice in the stone; the party's source for the devil's key weakness and the ally who pulls the Lictor's kobold screen in the Muster (Scene 7), met in the parley of Scene 6.
- **Wants:** To get her clutch's hatchlings *out* of the diggings before the "god-general" spends them all on its muster.
- **Secret:** Her clutch has haunted this quarry for generations as a threadbare garrison-cult — half-hearing the Voice's whisper, mangling its drill, pilfering tools and spooking cutters, being run off and breeding back. Only since the breach roared the Voice to full strength has the cult become an *army*, and she is the first to doubt it — terrified her own kind will call her a traitor. She knows the ward-line the Lictor won't cross, the murder-hole layout, and the secret the doubters live by: **the Voice weakens with distance and dies at the surface.**
- **Voice:** *"The Voice is not a god. Gods do not run out of soldiers. It is running out of us."*
- **Visual:** Small, soot-dark scales scarred pale along the spine, a filed-down Turathi bronze chit worn as a charm she's ashamed of; watches exits, never corners. **Casting (voice):** a whispery, quick Andy Serkis register; or Kathryn Hahn for dry nerve.

## Scenes

*Loose sequence: 1 is the hook, 2–4 the approach and delve, 5–8 the bunker. Combat scenes: 2 & 3 (kobolds), 4 & 7 (the Lictor arc — terror/puzzle, not kill-fights), 5 (the dead, conditional), 8 (the boss); Scene 6 is a social breather. Encounter budget for four 3rd-level PCs: a standard fight ≈ 4 points, where CR ½ = 1 pt, CR 1 = 2, CR 2 = 4, CR 3 = 6; kobolds (CR 1/8) = 0.2 each.*

### 1. Fallcrest — The Cartage Yard *(hook, no combat)*
- **Purpose:** Hire the party, plant both clocks, let them gear up. Establish that the quarrymaster is missing and the guard won't act.
- **Setting:** A muddy cartage yard under the Upper Quays — draft mules, stacked timber, a cramped office smelling of axle-grease and wet straw. The daughter runs it; she's holding it together on nerve. Late afternoon; a courier's just brought a fled cutter's garbled account.
- **At stake:** Whether the party takes the job, and on what terms. She **opens hard** — 50 gp now and *half* the vault, keeping the rest ("it's my family's quarry") — but it's a bluff; the moment they look ready to walk she folds to "all of it, just bring him back" (full deal and the aftermath that hard bargaining earns are in her NPC entry). She knows: carts stopped four days ago; a fled cutter raved about "kobolds that march" and "a voice in the stone"; her father's last note said he'd broken into "something big and old" that would set them right — but not *what*; he went up two nights ago and hasn't returned; and when she rode up after him yesterday, a pack of kobolds moving in ranks drove her back down the ridge before she reached the pit.
- **Ways in / ways out:** They arrive by referral (posted notice, a mutual contact, or a tavern tip). Out: straight to the Ridge Road, or an hour asking around Fallcrest first — an old quarryman or a Fallcrest sage (DC 12 History) recalls the Moon Hills hold "Turathi work" beneath them, sealed for good reason.
- **Complications:** The daughter wants to come along (liability, or a stubborn ally).
- **Sensory:** Rain ticking on a tin roof; the daughter's ink-stained hands; a mule screaming somewhere in the dark.

### 2. The Ridge Road *(warm-up fight)*
- **Purpose:** Teach terrain and telegraph the theme: *these are not normal kobolds.* Easy, winnable fight that establishes the enemy fights **drilled** — volleys, cover, orderly retreat.
- **Setting:** Switchback trail up Gray Ridge at dusk; loose scree, a timber flume, boulders good for cover on both sides. A cutter's abandoned handcart lies overturned across the path.
- **At stake:** Get up the ridge; take a prisoner or let one flee (a fleeing kobold *reports*, so Scene 3 is forewarned and better-organized).
- **Encounter:** **10 kobolds** (≈2 pts, deliberately easy). They are **enthralled, not evil** — driven, not willing. They fight to a *badly-remembered* Turathi drill (front rank slings, rear rank reloads), bark clipped Infernal parade-commands they only half-understand (*"Hold! Volley! Re-form!"*), and shout the sentry call-and-response at the walls: **"Sha Turath?" — "Turath vorel; abanne kesh!"** (an attentive party clocks the phrase; it's the **counter-sign**, load-bearing in Scene 5). **They break easily:** drop a few, or break the Voice's reach with distance or noise, and the rest **rout** rather than die to the last — a clear non-lethal option. Once, a kobold's eyes well over black mid-volley and it snaps a too-precise order in a voice not its own, then clears, streaked and blinking — the Voice riding it for a heartbeat. One wears a scrap of Turathi bronze as a rank-badge. **Clue:** this is real military doctrine, poorly copied — something *trained* them, and they answer to a "Voice."
- **Ways in / ways out:** Arrive on foot/mule up the trail. Out: over the crest into the open works (Scene 3). A kobold **spared or captured** (rather than beaten for answers) hisses one line before it bolts — *"Not all the clutch loves the Voice"* — and carries a **filed-down bronze chit**, the defectors' shame-mark; a party that kills everything still finds the chit on a body. Seeds the dissident payoff (Scene 6).
- **Complications:** A rockslide trap rigged at the flume (DC 13 Perception to spot; DC 13 Dex save, 2d10 bludgeoning, prone). The overturned cart hides **Renzo Calvi**, a wounded cutter playing dead — the only man to make it out of the mine.
- **Sensory:** Sling-stones cracking off marble; the drilled, wordless whistle-signals the kobolds use; cold pine wind off the heights.

### 3. Gray Ridge Open Works *(standard fight)*
- **Purpose:** The surface disaster laid bare; secure the pithead; learn the shape of what happened before descending.
- **Setting:** A stepped marble pit, two timber gantry cranes leaning at wrong angles, ore-cart rails running to a dark adit in the pit floor — the **Deep Gallery** mouth. A ransacked scale-house/office; a bunkhouse with the doors torn off. The kobolds hold the gallery mouth and the high benches.
- **At stake:** Take the pithead without the kobolds collapsing the gallery mouth (trapping the cutters below) or torching the office (which holds the quarrymaster's dig-journal — the map to the breach, and, scrawled in its margins, the Infernal watchword Donato heard the kobolds chanting: *"Sha Turath? — Turath vorel; abanne kesh"* — the **counter-sign**, with his guess that it's an old Bael Turath war-cry).
- **Encounter:** **1 Kobold Trapsmith (CR 1)** directing **10 kobolds** (≈4 pts, standard). Only the **trapsmith is a true fanatic** — it fights to the end to rig the gallery props to collapse; the other kobolds are the same enthralled, brittle drilled troops as Scene 2 and **rout** once the trapsmith drops or the Voice's grip is broken. The collapse is an *objective clock*: 3 rounds to stop it (destroy the charge, or drop the trapsmith). Multi-level terrain: benches give high ground and cover; crane counterweights can be cut loose (DC 13) to sweep a bench.
- **Ways in / ways out:** From the crest (Scene 2). Out: down the Deep Gallery (Scene 4). The office journal or a rescued cutter points the way and warns of "the door that isn't marble."
- **Complications:** A hidden **hatchling-nest** is tucked in the spoil-heaps behind the bunkhouse — eggs and a few terrified brood-tenders the muster was never told about, kept *outside the mine* where the Voice is weakest. The kobolds here fight *away* from it, trying not to draw its notice (a watchful party sees they're covering something). Smashing the nest sours the Scene 6 parley; sparing or guarding it earns a bonus.
- **Sensory:** White marble dust over everything like old snow; the groan of an off-balance crane; the skittering of kobolds you can't quite see among the benches.

### 4. The Deep Gallery & The Breach *(first contact with the Lictor — terror/gate, not a kill-fight)*
- **Purpose:** Transition from mundane mine to infernal ruin; the devil speaks for the first time; the party meets the Lictor and learns *damage won't kill it.*
- **Setting:** A timber-shored adit sloping down along the marble seam, ore-track underfoot, air warming and going metallic. It ends where clean white stone is smashed through into **black, fire-glazed Turathi masonry** — a carved infernal lintel, veins of dull-red conduit in the walls, a dormant **ward-glyph** on the threshold. Beyond: a guard antechamber where the Lictor is at work.
- **At stake:** Get past the Lictor and across the breach — alive, ideally with the captive it's about to execute. The devil's voice first reaches them here, calm and *wrong*: *"Sentries. Identify. You are late for the muster."*
- **Encounter — the Lictor** *(see Order of Battle):* the party arrives as the bronze executioner drags a bound, screaming cutter toward the conduit-forge to be killed and raised. Here there is **no way to stop it for good** — this beat is survival and dread, not the kill (that comes in Scene 7). It is nigh-unkillable and **holds the threshold — it will not cross the ward-line** (standing order). Outs: rescue the captive and fall back past the ward-line (it won't follow); bloody it to force a **withdrawal** and slip by; or duck through an escape the two-heads-tall bronze can't take — **a miner's crawl too tight for its bulk, or the cracked timber span that won't bear its weight.** Brute force *fails loudly* — that's the lesson. Bypassing the ward (DC 14 Arcana/Thieves' Tools) is quieter than triggering it (alarm — wakes Scene 5 early).
- **Ways in / ways out:** From the pit (Scene 3). Out: into the bunker proper — the War-Room (Scene 5) — with the Lictor now loose somewhere behind them.
- **Complications:** The conduit-veins pulse brighter each round the party lingers. A lone kobold cowers away from the Lictor and slips off deeper the instant it's seen — a glimpse of the doubters (parley in Scene 6). If the captive dies, the party watches the devil raise it on the spot — horror, and proof of the stakes.
- **Sensory:** Heat rising like an oven cracked open; hot bronze and old ash; the Lictor's dead-silent tread, then the shriek of the man it drags; the devil's voice narrating the execution as routine paperwork.

### 5. The War-Room *(the interrogation — clue nexus + conditional fight)*
- **Purpose:** The centerpiece of the *idea*: the party stands inside the decayed mind and is *processed* by it. The single richest clue-station in the adventure, and the setup for the finale's choice.
- **Setting:** A long vaulted hall around a great **cast-bronze relief map** of the Nentir Vale as it was in the war — markers for legions centuries dead, some pins moved *recently.* Walls of standing-orders etched in Infernal; murder-holes above; barred holding cells to one side; the conduit gallery rising away at the far end. The Voice owns this room.
- **The interrogation:** The moment they enter, the Haruspex addresses them through the stone (*"Report received…"*, after that half-second relay delay) as *sentries reporting late for muster* and runs them through the full protocol — **identify your unit; report status; give the counter-sign; explain your delay.** It asks all four questions, *then* classifies once and acts — it won't interrogate indefinitely. Their answers set their **classification:**
  - **Reinforcements** (play it straight — military bearing, Infernal or Turathi tokens, a plausible unit; the *counter-sign* seals it): conscripted. It assigns them "a sector to hold" and **orders them toward the throne** — they walk deeper as honored guests, no fight.
  - **Infiltrators** (contradict it, refuse, or fail the counter-sign): condemned — the room turns hostile (labor-dead attack, murder-holes open).
  - **Unverified** (partial): suspicion — a trick question or a second demand for the counter-sign; one more exchange decides it.
  - **The counter-sign** — *"Sha Turath? — Turath vorel; abanne kesh"* (an old Bael Turath war-cry from the War of Ruin) — is a watchword only the garrison knows, so the party has to have gathered it earlier: the **kobolds' parade-chant** (Scenes 2–3), **Donato's dig-journal** (Scene 3 office), or **Renzo** (Scene 2). Failing that, they can **bluff** (Deception vs. its Insight). Rewards earlier clue-work.
- **What the room reveals (clue-stations).** *Rank these so the scene can't idle: the GM volunteers the two **must-surface** stations even to a passive party; the rest reward looking.*
  - **The war-map** *(must-surface)* → its mission and target: a marker in Infernal sits exactly on **Fallcrest**, and recently-moved pins show it *is acting now.*
  - **The Centurion's regalia** *(must-surface)* → Donato's coat and tools laid out as a commander's dress — the father is at the throne, being made into something.
  - **The standing-orders wall** *(on a check — read Infernal, DC 14 Arcana/History)* → it holds the *Haruspex* rank, its commander is dead, and it **awaits relief from duty by proper authority** — the seed of the peaceful ending.
  - **The holding cells** *(optional)* → the **surviving trapped cutters you came for** — this is where they're saved; free them here (loud, but it's the rescue). Every night the party stops to rest, one more captive is dragged off to be processed.
  - **The labor-dead** *(optional)* → a PC recognizes a face, one of Renzo's crew; the dead are called by rank, and when one pauses to relay an order its eyes well black and streak with cursed-tears (horror).
- **Encounter (if hostile):** **6 reanimated cutters (Zombie, CR 1/4 ≈3 pts)** — the *strong* dead (dwarves, orcs), directed by **2 kobold overseers** (kobolds the minds, the dead the muscle). The dead never rout and can be *ordered into the conduit-arc* to wall off a lane, burning without flinching (see Order of Battle). Terrain: the map-table is cover and high ground; murder-holes let unseen kobolds throw stones (the Voice "calling fire").
- **Ways in / ways out:** From the breach (Scene 4). Branches: (a) pass as reinforcements → *ordered* toward the throne; (b) fight through; (c) slip for the side-tunnels to find the doubting clutch (Scene 6). Out: the Doubting Clutch (Scene 6), the Muster (Scene 7), or — if classified reinforcements — marched straight toward the Throne (Scene 8). **A party marched straight past Scenes 6–7 doesn't skip them for free:** as it moves through the conduit gallery, **Ssvara's clutch intercepts it** — a whispered *"not here — the Voice hears — come, we talk"* — offering the Scene 6 parley (and its key weakness and intel) rather than letting a social party arrive at the throne blind. A group that waves her off *can* still win at the throne, but the harder way (see Scene 8).
- **Complications:** If they pass as reinforcements, the Haruspex keeps *testing* them en route (a sudden order to prove obedience). A freed captive may panic and blow their cover.
- **Sensory:** The dead-air reek of a room sealed for centuries; the scrape of the labor-dead dragging ore; the Voice everywhere and nowhere, unhurried, certain, pausing as if *listening to a commander who never answers.*

### 6. The Doubting Clutch *(social — no combat; the dissident payoff)*
- **Purpose:** Cash in the kobold seeds; hand the party the *key clue* for beating the devil; optionally recruit aid for the Muster. A breather between the War-Room and the assault.
- **Setting:** A **conduit-dead pocket** — a side-gallery where the infernal circuitry is broken and the Voice fades to static. That is *why* the dissidents gather here: it is the one spot inside the works the Voice cannot fully compel them. Ssvara's clutch shelters here; their eggs are stashed safer still, up in the open works *outside the mine*, because the doubters have learned the Voice weakens with distance and dies at the surface.
- **At stake:** Trust — whether Ssvara helps, and how much. **Open gate:** any party can earn her aid through good roleplay in the moment; there is no hard lock. But prior conduct sets her starting attitude — mercy to kobolds and sparing the hatchling-nest start her *helpful*; a genocidal path starts her *terrified and hostile*, and she'll demand the party first swear and *prove* safe passage for the hatchlings before she gives anything.
- **What she gives (scaled to trust):**
  - **The key weakness (always, if they parley at all):** *the Voice weakens with distance from the throne and dies outside the mine; the conduit carries its command.* This is how you win — **cut the conduit, or get Donato out of the works,** and the devil's hold breaks.
  - **Intel (helpful attitude):** the throne-room layout, the murder-hole and ward-line map, where the conduit can be cut in the Muster (Scene 7), and the truth about Donato — *"the Voice wears your quarrymaster now; it's making him its Centurion."*
  - **Aid in the Muster (trust earned):** her clutch opens a blast-door and pulls the Lictor's kobold screen, turning the showdown from a brutal race into a winnable one (Scene 7).
- **Ways in / ways out:** Sought after the War-Room (Scene 5), once the party grasps what the Voice is; the captured chit (Scene 2) or a freed captive points the way. Out: the Muster (Scene 7), now forewarned — or, if the party promised it, a detour to walk the hatchlings up to the surface first.
- **Complications:** A loyalist kobold overhears and bolts to report — a chase to silence it, or the Muster comes at full alert. Ssvara's own nerve can crack: if the party is cold or threatening, she may betray them to prove her loyalty to the clutch.
- **Sensory:** The Voice guttering to static in the dead pocket; the dry rustle of frightened kobolds; a faint draft of *outside air* telling everyone the surface — and safety — is close.

### 7. The Muster *(the Lictor climax — trap the unstoppable, cripple the boss)*
- **Purpose:** Pay off the dungeon-long dread: here the party takes the Lictor *permanently* off the board — not with damage but by sealing it away — and cripples the devil on the way to the throne.
- **Setting:** The **conduit gallery** — a rising warren of glowing infernal circuitry linking War-Room to Throne. Its heart is a **fortified conduit junction**, a raised platform the garrison holds, framed by two heavy **blast-doors** on either side of the corridor. The junction is *not* the only way through: cramped **maintenance crawls, a flanking service-gallery, and a half-collapsed stope** offer avenues *around* the killbox for a party that scouts (Ssvara's intel, or DC 13 Perception/Investigation). The central junction can be **cut** (DC 14 tools/Arcana): cutting it staggers the devil — halves its HP and ends *call the reserve* in Scene 8 — *and* blocks the free-it ending. A cold thread of *outside air* pulls uphill through the crawls.
- **At stake:** Get through with the Lictor dealt with. Sensing the threat, the devil finally **commits the Lictor to the kill** and stops hoarding it.
- **Dealing with the Lictor (preferred + alternates):** The intended solve is a *trap*, not a fight: **lure or funnel the Lictor into the corridor between the two blast-doors, drop both doors, and jam or wreck the mechanisms** (DC 14 tools/Arcana, or destroy the door-winches) so the Haruspex can't reopen them — sealing it away for the rest of the adventure. Alternates for a party that improvises: drive it up a crawl toward the **surface** (the Voice dies, it goes inert), or drop it in the **arc-forge**. A straight damage-race still loses.
- **Encounter — the Lictor, unleashed** + a thin screen of **kobolds and anchoring dead** holding the junction and working the door-winches (the dead plug the doorways, the kobolds funnel). A *puzzle under pressure*: work the doors and the ground while the screen buys the devil time. If the party earned Ssvara's aid (Scene 6), her clutch seizes a winch and pulls the kobold screen, turning a brutal race into a winnable one.
- **Ways in / ways out:** From the Doubting Clutch (Scene 6) or straight from the War-Room (Scene 5). Out: the Command Throne (Scene 8) — the boss already crippled if the junction was cut here.
- **Complications:** A blast-door slams early and splits the party for a round (teach cross-fire) — or is exactly the tool they turn against the Lictor. If the party skipped Scene 6 and never learned the fix, let Renzo's half-memory or the pulsing veins hint at the doors — don't let the Lictor become an unsolvable wall. The devil narrates it all like a commander spending his reserves, praising "good order."
- **Sensory:** Red conduit-light strobing; the Lictor's bronze tread closing in the dark; ozone and cooking dust; the boom and grind of blast-doors dropping.

### 8. The Command Throne *(finale; con / fight / free-it all supported)*
- **Purpose:** Resolve the war. Three real paths, each mechanically supported.
- **Setting:** A domed command chamber; the Haruspex fused to a **bronze command-throne** grown into the living stone, ringed by a lower map-pit of the whole front. Beside it stands the **quarrymaster**, half-made into the Centurion — pallid, eyes welling black and streaked with cursed-tears, echoing the Haruspex's cadence, still fighting it underneath. Conduit-veins feed the throne from every wall — and the **conduit junction can be cut here too** (DC 14 tools/Arcana), exactly as in Scene 7, halving the Haruspex's HP and ending *call the reserve* for a party that never crippled it on the way in. The **Sigil of the Ninth Reach** — the command-seal the stand-down needs — is fused to the throne's own regalia, clearly *wielded* by the Haruspex and pointedly *withheld* from Donato; it can be seized mid-scene (an action, or a contested Athletics / Sleight of Hand check). If the Lictor wasn't sealed away in Scene 7, it looms at the chamber's edge (**mark it on the map**) and closes in if the fight runs long.
- **At stake:** The Haruspex's fate, the father's soul, and whether the thing ever reaches Fallcrest.
- **The three paths:**
  - **CON (intended clever win).** The Haruspex is *bound to obey command authority* — it cannot act, or stand down, without a commander's order. It is lucid on procedure and insane on reality, so "you lost" never works; only a **correctly-formed relief-of-post order** does. The order has four elements — **Authority + Counter-sign + Form + Token** — which the party assembles from what it gathered; the exact resolution (**each element brought = one automatic success; three elements end it in the party's favor with no rolls; a missing element must be substituted with a DC 13–15 check, and a failed substitution is a failure; missing Authority cannot be rolled past**) lives in **The stand-down protocol**, GM Notes. Two keys supply **Authority**: **(a)** produce or forge a superior it recognizes — a Turathi command-seal, or a cease-fire order in Infernal (DC 15 Deception/Forgery); or **(b) free Donato and have him, as the Haruspex's own installed Centurion, give the order** (fusing the rescue and the win). **Freeing Donato is path-agnostic:** his own voice surfaces when the party invokes **Serica or the yard** — doing so is itself a valid CON success that breaks the Voice's grip (for talkers) — *or* he can be wrenched loose in the fight (grapple + DC 13 Wis-save aid; see FIGHT). Either way a freed Donato supplies Authority. Success: it stands down, releases Donato, powers the bunker to sleep — the relief it has craved for centuries. Failure: it brands them infiltrators — roll to FIGHT with the garrison already engaged.
  - **FIGHT (heroic win).** The Haruspex is **anchored** (can't leave the throne) — spec ~CR 3–4: multiattack glaive/beard reskin, **standing-orders aura** (one ally reaction-attack/round), **hellish rebuke**, and once/fight *call the reserve* (2 lemures / 2 kobolds rise from the map-pit). Throne-room hazards: conduit-arcs deal 2d6 fire (DC 13 Dex) in marked squares, but they **fire on a telegraphed cycle — the veins flare bright a beat before each discharge** — so a party that reads the room can time its lanes and a *clever* group should be able to avoid them entirely. The **half-made Centurion (Donato)** acts on the Haruspex's initiative unless freed; **breaking Donato free** (grapple + DC 13 Wis save aid, or reduce the Haruspex's control) removes the devil's action-economy edge — the mechanical reward for caring about the rescue. Cutting the throne-conduit (Scene 7 or here) halves the Haruspex's HP and ends its *call the reserve.* **Soft floor (this is an intro module):** the Haruspex craves relief and *classifies before it kills* — it opens by demanding surrender and interrogating rather than alpha-striking, and if it drops a PC it **pauses to offer terms** rather than pressing for a wipe, leaving a losing party an off-ramp into the CON. Deadly, but not a quiet TPK.
  - **FREE-IT (the trap).** The Haruspex promises the father's release, treasure, "safe passage" if the party relinks its supply line (repair the conduit / carry its "orders" to the surface). Doing so wins the fight instantly and *loses the adventure*: the Haruspex marches on Fallcrest with the party as its unwitting vanguard. Reserve this as a consequence, not a gotcha — telegraph the cost.
- **Ways in / ways out:** From the Muster (Scene 7) or straight from the War-Room if the party talked its way in. Out: father saved and **bunker re-sealed** (best) — after the Voice is relieved or destroyed, re-inscribe the threshold ward-glyph (DC 14 Arcana) *or* collapse the deep gallery with the Scene 3 charges / crane rig, entombing the Ninth Reach; **Haruspex destroyed** but the bunker left open (good); or **Haruspex freed** (dark — sequel hook).
- **Complications:** The father lucid for one round, begging them to *end him* if they can't free him.
- **Sensory:** The throne's bronze skin sweating heat; the father's voice sliding between his own and the devil's; the map-pit lighting up with the ghost-order of a battle four centuries lost.

## The Ninth Reach — bunker map & area key

A large Bael Turath forward command bunker, sealed and derelict for four centuries and gutted by Arkhosian dragon-fire in the War of Ruin. Most of it is dead — dust, collapse, standing water — and only the **command spine** (War-Room → Conduit Gallery → Throne) still "lives," lit by the conduit the Haruspex rides. The eight scenes sit inside this larger ruin; the **lettered areas are optional and mostly non-combat**, there to make the place feel vast and to feed the stand-down (Authority, Form). Impassable wings are flagged so exploration doesn't eat the session.

*State keys: **live** (conduit-lit, the Voice reaches) · **derelict** (intact but dead) · **collapsed** (impassable) · **flooded** · **sealed** (undisturbed).*

- **Gray Ridge Open Works** *(surface, S3)* — the mundane pithead and dig. **Exits:** down the Deep Gallery.
- **1 · The Deep Gallery** — timber-shored mine adit driven down the marble seam to the Turathi wall. **State:** unstable. **Exits:** up to the Open Works; down to the Breach.
- **2 · The Breach & Guard Antechamber** *(S4)* — where white marble is smashed into black fire-glazed masonry; the dormant **ward-line** threshold; the Lictor's gate. **State:** fracture, warded. **Exits:** up the gallery; ┄ward┄ into the War-Room; a corridor to the Barracks (A); the concourse opens onto the Crater (B).
- **A · The Garrison Barracks** *(non-combat)* — rows of bunks under four centuries of dust, a dead legionary's kit and an unsent letter home. **State:** derelict. **Exits:** the Breach concourse; a caved passage toward the Crater (B). **Beat:** a mournful lore stop — who the Ninth Reach's men were, that they were sealed in *alive*; minor period loot.
- **B · The War-of-Ruin Crater** *(non-combat / dead-end)* — a whole wing sheared open by Arkhosian dragon-fire, floor gone to a black chasm; the wound that killed the post. **State:** collapsed / impassable. **Exits:** the Barracks (A) and Breach concourse; across the chasm (roped traverse, DC 14 Athletics) a sealed vault-cell most tables never open. **Beat:** an awe/dread vista that sells the scale and the War of Ruin.
- **5 · The War-Room** *(S5, hub)* — the decayed mind: bronze relief-map, Infernal standing-orders walls, murder-holes, holding cells. The most "alive" room. **State:** live. **Exits:** ┄ward┄ back to the Breach; the cells; a door to the Auspices Chamber (C); side-tunnels to the Doubting Clutch (6); a stair down to the Flooded Sublevel (D); the conduit gallery rising to the Muster (7).
- **Holding cells** *(part of S5)* — barred cells holding the surviving trapped cutters — the rescue. **State:** live.
- **C · The Auspices Chamber** *(non-combat, lore)* — the Haruspex's sanctum-annex: a dry scrying-basin, omen-bronze, entrail-charts of a war long lost. **State:** cracked, seeping. **Exits:** off the War-Room. **Beat:** reading the omen-bronze (DC 14 Arcana/Religion, or anyone who reads Infernal) yields the exact **form** of a relief-of-post order — a direct key to the stand-down protocol.
- **6 · The Doubting Clutch (Dead Pocket)** *(S6, social)* — a side-gallery where the conduit is broken and the Voice guts to static; Ssvara's clutch shelters here. **State:** broken conduit (safe pocket). **Exits:** side-tunnels from the War-Room; a **fissure to the surface** (proof the Voice dies outside; the escape, and where the hatchlings are walked out); on to the Muster approach (7). **Beat:** the parley; the key weakness handed over.
- **D · The Flooded Sublevel** *(non-combat, exploration/hazard)* — drowned storerooms and the garrison cistern below the War-Room; cold, black, waist-to-neck deep. **State:** flooded. **Exits:** stair down from the War-Room; a submerged crawl that **flanks into the Muster past a blast-door** (hold-breath, DC 13). **Beat:** salvage; the draft and current confirm the surface is close.
- **7 · The Conduit Gallery / The Muster** *(S7)* — the rising fortified spine: the central **conduit junction**, two **blast-doors**, and flanking **maintenance crawls / a collapsed stope**. **State:** live (partial collapse at the edges). **Exits:** from the War-Room (main), the Dead Pocket (6), or the Flooded crawl (D); the Arc-Forge (11) opens off it; ══blast-doors══ up to the Throne (8).
- **11 · The Arc-Forge** *(hazard)* — the conduit-forge where captives are killed and raised; roaring heat and arcs. **State:** live/active. **Exits:** off the Conduit Gallery. **Note:** a terminal hazard — drop the Lictor in here to stop it for good.
- **E · The Legate's Quarters** *(non-combat, major payoff)* — the dead commander's sealed rooms off the throne approach; his mummified remains still at his desk, his **command-seal** and a **sealed dispatch-cylinder** with him. **State:** sealed tomb, undisturbed. **Exits:** off the throne approach. **Beat:** genuine **Authority** for the stand-down (his seal beats a forgery), the form/counter-sign in his papers, and the sequel hook (a superior still unrelieved; a second command-post).
- **8 · The Command Throne** *(S8, finale)* — the domed command chamber; the Haruspex fused to the throne over a map-pit of the whole front. **State:** live (the heart). **Exits:** up from the Muster; the Legate's Quarters (E) and the Reliquary (14) open off it.
- **14 · The Reliquary** *(strongroom)* — the Ninth Reach hoard behind the throne (see Vault loot). **State:** sealed. **Exits:** off the Throne.

## GM Notes

- **The escalation clock (kept simple):** the delve is one continuous push, so the *only* thing that advances the threat is the party choosing to **take a long rest inside the works.** Default starting state (a breach a few days old): the Lictor prowls, the war-room is manned, Donato is captured and half-made. Each **long rest** the party takes, apply one step of escalation — another captured cutter is processed into the labor-dead (one fewer to free in the cells); the garrison goes to a higher alert; and Donato's conversion advances (after a second rest he fights at *full strength* in Scene 8). No passive night-by-night bookkeeping — resting is simply a real, legible trade.
- **Talking to the boss:** The Haruspex is a *conversation*, not just a statblock. Lucid on procedure, insane on reality. Play it as a diviner-counselor relaying a field report to a commander who isn't there — it cannot command or stand down on its own, and will not accept "you lost," only a *correctly-formed order from proper authority* (a forged superior, or Donato freed and commanding). That gap is the whole finale.
- **Clue spine (what the party can learn, and where).** Each fact is findable in at least two places, so no single miss blocks the finale. Reward attention; don't gate anything behind a lone die roll.

| # | What they can learn | Where to get it |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **The place:** a sealed Bael Turath command bunker; the crew broke into something old and bound. | Fallcrest lore (S1, DC 12); Donato's dig-journal (S3 office); the worked infernal stone at the breach (S4). |
| 2 | **The Voice:** one intelligence commands the kobolds and the dead; it speaks from the stone and calls its troops by rank. | Drilled kobolds who "answer to a Voice" (S2); Renzo's testimony (S2+); first direct contact (S4). |
| 3 | **Its nature & target:** a bound infernal counselor re-fighting the War of Ruin; its commander is centuries dead; it means to march on **Fallcrest.** | The war-map + Fallcrest marker (S5); the standing-orders wall (S5, DC 14); the interrogation (S5). |

- **Difficulty dials:** Softer — the Lictor withdraws at a higher HP threshold and gets an extra telegraphed out, fewer kobolds in Scene 3, skip the Scene 2 trap. Harder — a *second*, half-functional Lictor; start the garrison at full alert with Donato already fully converted (fights at full strength in Scene 8); add 2 lemures to the throne room.
- **Encounter budget (four 3rd-level PCs; standard ≈ 4 pts):** S2 ~2 pts (easy), S3 ~4 (standard), S4 the Lictor gate (terror/puzzle — not an XP kill), S5 ~3 (moderate, conditional), S6 social (no combat), S7 the Lictor climax (puzzle under pressure + thin screen), S8 boss ~CR 3–4 anchored (deadly, winnable). Kobold swarms now live only in S2–S3; S4/S7 are the Lictor, S5 the dead — deliberate variety.
- **If they seal it and leave (the dark shortcut):** once the party learns the Voice is anchored and dies at the surface, a mercenary group may skip the throne entirely — collapse the gallery or re-inscribe the ward from the safe side and walk. Support it rather than block it, but make the cost explicit: this **buys time, it doesn't win.** It abandons Donato, the trapped cutters, and the vault (→ Serica's *father dead + they got paid* → **lasting enemy** aftermath), and the sealed garrison of kobolds and labor-dead eventually digs its way out — the Haruspex, still working to install a commander, later leads that army on Fallcrest. That's a future adventure (a string of sidetracks, then an invasion), not a clean ending.
- **Safety tools:** Body-horror in the father's conversion and the reanimated cutters — flag lines/veils in Session 0; the conversion can be dialed to "enthralled, not transformed" if needed.

### The stand-down protocol
The bunker is Bael Turath's **Ninth Reach**, and its Haruspex can only be shut down by a properly-executed **relief-of-post order** — it cannot be argued out of the war, only *relieved* from it. A valid order has four parts; the party assembles them from what they've gathered, and each part they bring turns a step of the CON challenge in their favor.

1. **Authority — *who* relieves it.** Only two things count: **(a) Donato**, freed and acting as its own installed Centurion, giving the order; or **(b) a superior it recognizes** — a forged high-command order in Infernal (DC 15 Deception/Forgery) or a presented Turathi command-seal. **No authority = no stand-down**, however smooth the talk.
2. **The counter-sign** — *"Sha Turath? — Turath vorel; abanne kesh"* — the Bael Turath war-cry the party gathered before Scene 5 (the kobold parade-chant, Donato's dig-journal, or Renzo). Proves the order is legitimate, not enemy trickery.
3. **The correct form.** The relief must be spoken in protocol: name it by rank (*Haruspex of the Ninth Reach*), cite the standing order it rescinds, and formally relieve it of the post. Known from the standing-orders wall (Scene 5), Donato's dig-journal, or a History/Arcana check (or anyone who reads Infernal).
4. **A token of relief.** The ritual closes on a physical token pressed to the throne — the **Sigil of the Ninth Reach**, which the Haruspex openly *wields* at the throne and pointedly withholds from Donato; the party can **seize it during Scene 8** (an action, or a contested Athletics / Sleight of Hand check). Lacking it, **Donato's own hand as Centurion** serves.

**Running it (the CON resolution):** the four elements are **Authority, Counter-sign, Form, and Token.** **Each element the party actually brings = one automatic success.** Bring **three or more** and the relief lands outright with no rolls — doing it *correctly* is the whole point. For each element they *lack*, they may attempt a **DC 13–15 substitution** (bluff the counter-sign, improvise the form, press Donato's bare hand in place of the Sigil); a **failed substitution is a failure,** and **two failures** brands them infiltrators (→ FIGHT). **Missing Authority cannot be rolled past** — no forged superior and no freed Donato means no stand-down, however smooth the talk.

**When it works** (read-aloud cue): the Voice stops mid-relay — for the first time in four centuries it is addressed by its own chain of command. *"Haruspex of the Ninth Reach — your standing orders are rescinded. The front is dissolved. You are relieved."* And it answers, almost grateful: *"…Acknowledged. Relieved."* The conduit dims, the bunker powers to sleep, and Donato drops free.

**Failure / refusal:** a malformed order, missing authority, or a fumbled counter-sign reads to it as enemy disinformation — it brands them infiltrators and the FIGHT begins with the garrison already up.

### Vault loot
The "something big and old" Donato found. Most of it sits in a strongroom off the throne chamber (Scene 8), recovered after the finale — the **Sigil is the exception**, wielded by the Haruspex and grabbable *during* the finale (see the stand-down protocol). The bulk is **heavy bronze wealth** — the thing that actually saves the Aldabri family, and what Serica's split is fighting over (her deal governs the party's cut).

- **The Ninth Reach hoard** — Bael Turath bronze scrip, ingots, and old coin, ~750 gp all told but a full *cart-load* to move (fittingly, the Aldabri run cartage). This is what "sets the family right."
- **Infernal-cut gems** — two or three, ~150 gp, light and pocketable. Deliberate temptation: a party that quietly skims these is short-changing Serica (feeds her aftermath matrix).
- **Sigil of the Ninth Reach** *(uncommon wondrous; also the stand-down token)* — a bronze command-seal, **taken from the Haruspex's own throne-regalia during Scene 8** (see the stand-down protocol), not the strongroom. 1/day, as an action, utter a one-word command in Infernal that a **bound Bael Turath creature** (a construct or lesser devil — the Lictor, a lemure) must obey (Wis save DC 13 negates). **No power over the Haruspex itself.**
- **Officer's spatha of the Ninth Reach** *(uncommon, +1 sword)* — infernal-bronze; sheds dim red light in 10 ft and flares brighter when a fiend is within 60 ft (a built-in warning).
- **Legionary's gorget** *(uncommon wondrous)* — infernal-bronze collar granting **resistance to fire** (handy against conduit-arcs); as an alt, advantage on saves vs. being frightened.
- **A sealed dispatch-cylinder** *(sequel hook; minor value)* — Infernal orders referencing a **superior still unrelieved** and a *second* command-post elsewhere in the Vale. Doubles as a source of the correct "form" (and a forgeable superior's hand) for the stand-down.
- *Optional consumables:* 2 potions of fire resistance and one spell scroll (GM's choice, 1st–2nd level) in an officer's field-kit.
