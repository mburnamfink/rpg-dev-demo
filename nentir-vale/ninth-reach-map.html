The Ninth ReachBael Turath forward command bunker — sealed four centuries

A sprawling command post, gutted by Arkhosian dragon-fire in the War of Ruin and dark ever since. Most of it is dead stone, standing water, and collapse. Only the command spine — War-Room to Conduit Gallery to Throne — still lives, lit by the conduit the Haruspex rides. The eight scenes sit inside this larger ruin; lettered wings are optional and mostly non-combat. Tap any chamber to read its key.

Gray Ridge Open Works

Scene 3surface

The mundane pithead and marble dig — where the whole thing started. Timber cranes, ore-cart rails, the open sky.

Exits Down the Deep Gallery.

The Deep Gallery

derelict

Timber-shored mine adit driven down the marble seam until it struck the black Turathi wall. The descent from the living world into the ruin.

State Unstable shoring. Exits Up to the Open Works; down to the Breach.

2

The Breach & Guard Antechamber

Scene 4warded

Where clean white marble is smashed through into black, fire-glazed Turathi masonry — a carved infernal lintel, the dormant ward-line on the threshold, and the Lictor at work beyond it.

State Fracture point, warded. Exits Up the gallery; ward-line into the War-Room; a corridor to the Barracks; the concourse opens onto the Crater.

A

Garrison Barracks

derelict

Rows of bunks under four centuries of undisturbed dust; a dead legionary's kit still folded, an unsent letter home. Intact, but long dead.

State Derelict. Exits The Breach concourse; a caved passage toward the Crater.

◇ non-combat — lore: who the Ninth Reach's men were, and that they were sealed in alive; minor period loot
B

War-of-Ruin Crater

collapsed

A whole wing sheared open by Arkhosian dragon-fire, its floor gone to a black chasm — the wound that killed the post. Mostly a dead-end that sells the scale of the disaster.

State Collapsed / impassable. Exits Barracks & Breach concourse; across the chasm (roped, DC 14 Athletics) a sealed vault-cell few tables ever open.

◇ non-combat — an awe/dread vista of the War of Ruin
5

The War-Room

Scene 5live

The decayed mind itself: a great cast-bronze relief-map of the Vale, walls of Infernal standing-orders, murder-holes, barred cells. The hub, and the most "alive" room in the complex — the Voice owns it.

State Live. Exits Ward-line back to the Breach; the cells; the Auspices Chamber; side-tunnels to the Doubting Clutch; a stair down to the Flooded Sublevel; the conduit gallery rising to the Muster.

✚

Holding Cells

live

Barred cells off the War-Room holding the surviving trapped cutters — this is where the rescue actually happens. Loud to open, but it's the point.

Exits Off the War-Room.

C

Auspices Chamber

derelict

The Haruspex's sanctum-annex: a dry scrying-basin, omen-bronze, entrail-charts of a war long lost. Cracked and seeping, but readable.

State Cracked, seeping. Exits Off the War-Room.

◇ non-combat — reading the omen-bronze (DC 14 Arcana/Religion, or any Infernal reader) yields the exact FORM of a relief-of-post order → a direct key to the stand-down
6

The Doubting Clutch

Scene 6dead pocket

A side-gallery where the conduit is broken and the Voice guts to static — which is exactly why Ssvara's frightened clutch shelters here. A fissure lets in a thread of outside air.

State Broken conduit (safe pocket). Exits Side-tunnels from the War-Room; a fissure to the SURFACE (the Voice dies outside — an escape, and where the hatchlings are walked out); on to the Muster approach.

◇ non-combat — the parley; the key weakness handed over
D

Flooded Sublevel

flooded

Drowned storerooms and the garrison cistern below the War-Room; cold, black, waist-to-neck deep. Centuries of groundwater have claimed it.

State Flooded. Exits Stair down from the War-Room; a submerged crawl that flanks into the Muster past a blast-door (hold-breath, DC 13).

◇ non-combat — salvage; the draft and current prove the surface is close
7

The Conduit Gallery — The Muster

Scene 7live

The rising fortified spine: a central conduit junction on a defensible platform, two heavy blast-doors, and flanking maintenance crawls and a half-collapsed stope offering ways around the killbox. Cut the junction to cripple the boss; trap the Lictor between the doors to end it.

State Live (collapse at the edges). Exits From the War-Room, the Dead Pocket, or the Flooded crawl; the Arc-Forge opens off it; blast-doors up to the Throne.

11

The Arc-Forge

active

The conduit-forge where captives are killed and raised as labor-dead — roaring heat and lashing arcs of infernal current.

State Live / active. Exits Off the Conduit Gallery. Use A terminal hazard — pitch the Lictor in here to stop it for good.

E

The Legate's Quarters

sealed

The dead commander's sealed rooms off the throne approach; his mummified remains still at his desk, his command-seal and a sealed dispatch-cylinder beside him. Undisturbed since the sealing.

State Sealed tomb. Exits Off the throne approach.

◇ non-combat — genuine AUTHORITY for the stand-down (his seal beats a forgery), the form/counter-sign in his papers, and the sequel hook
8

The Command Throne

Scene 8live

The domed command chamber; the Haruspex fused to a bronze throne over a lower map-pit of the whole front, the half-made Centurion at its side. The heart of the ruin — and the finale.

State Live (the heart). Exits Up from the Muster; the Legate's Quarters and the Reliquary open off it.

14

The Reliquary

sealed

The strongroom behind the throne — the Ninth Reach hoard: cart-loads of heavy bronze wealth, infernal-cut gems, and officer's gear.

State Sealed. Exits Off the Throne.

The Quarry That Woke Up · GM reference. The command spine (War-Room → Muster → Throne) is the only route that must be run; every lettered wing is optional colour, atmosphere, or a stand-down key. Impassable/flooded wings are marked so the ruin feels vast without eating the session.
